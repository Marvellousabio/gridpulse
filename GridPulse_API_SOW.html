01

Executive Summary

A software-defined energy network for a market where the energy itself is the bottleneck.

Nigeria's transition away from petrol is no longer aspirational — it is being forced by economics. The 2023 removal of the fuel subsidy stripped away an estimated ₦4–5 trillion in annual under-recovery and reshaped the cost structure of every commercial rider, logistics fleet, and ride-hail operator in Lagos[11]. Electric two- and three-wheelers are the natural beneficiary, yet the constraint has simply moved one layer down the stack: the vehicles are ready, but the energy under them is not.

The physical energy layer that powers electric mobility in Lagos is volatile and fragmented. The national grid is intermittent; operators independently bolt on solar PV and diesel gensets as fallback[6]; battery-swap cabinets and a nascent class of off-grid hydrogen canister hubs run as isolated islands with no shared visibility, no demand forecasting, and no way to settle energy between competitors. The result is the worst of both worlds: empty stations next to congested ones, stranded fleets during grid outages, and capital locked in assets that cannot be shared.

GridPulse API is the middleware that resolves this. It is an API-first energy orchestration layer that abstracts heterogeneous physical assets — standard Lithium-Ion swap cabinets and pressurized hydrogen canister hubs — into one unified, polymorphic state model on AWS. A stateful multi-agent system built on LangGraph and orchestrated through Cencori continuously forecasts depletion, rebalances load across energy sources, and — critically — autonomously reroutes mixed EV fleets across operators and across chemistries when a localized grid failure is detected, without waiting for a human. An elizaOS-based machine-to-machine settlement ledger clears the resulting cross-operator energy debts cryptographically, against verifiable IoT hardware proof, and maintains an immutable record of clean-energy consumption in kWh/MJ-equivalent terms.

The thesis in one line: GridPulse turns energy from an asset each operator owns and hoards into a service the network routes — across incompatible chemistries, across competing operators, autonomously, and with cryptographic settlement that makes sharing safe.

Value proposition. For B2B fleet operators, GridPulse converts unpredictable downtime into a guaranteed-availability SLA. For third-party infrastructure franchise owners, it turns an idle swap cabinet or hydrogen hub into a revenue-generating node in a shared, settled marketplace. For institutional clean-energy investors, the immutable kWh/MJ ledger produces auditable, machine-verifiable evidence suitable for carbon-credit issuance and green-bond use-of-proceeds reporting — collateral that fragmented, paper-based operators cannot currently produce.

Problem Alignment Matrix

Direct, line-by-line mapping of GridPulse capabilities to the three hackathon problem statements.

Problem Statement The Lagos Failure Mode The GridPulse Mechanism
PS-1 — No Charging Intelligence Layer Operators (battery-swap, solar hub, hydrogen) run as disconnected islands. No national view of where energy is, what it costs, or whether a node is alive. Charging infrastructure remains sparse and uncoordinated[9]. GridPulse is the unified aggregation & visibility plane. Every node — regardless of operator or chemistry — streams a normalized telemetry envelope into one queryable state model, exposing a single national availability API across all assets.
PS-6 — Battery-Swap Demand Imbalance Riders arrive at empty cabinets while neighbouring stations sit full. Demand is spiky, commute-driven, and unforecast — producing stock-outs and idle inventory simultaneously. The LangGraph forecasting agent predicts per-cluster depletion ahead of the spike and pre-positions inventory and reroutes fleets before stock-out, shifting the system from reactive to predictive. Demand is modelled per cluster, not per station.
PS-9 — Grid Unreliability / Multi-Source Grid drops without warning; each operator independently and manually flips to solar or diesel[6]. A grid failure in one cluster strands every fleet that depends on it. On a detected grid-failure event, the agentic loop autonomously orchestrates the source shift (grid → solar → hydrogen reserve) and reroutes affected fleets to healthy cross-chemistry nodes — turning a local outage into a contained, self-healing event.

Why all three at once: these are not three problems — they are one problem viewed from three angles. You cannot predict swap demand (PS-6) without a unified data layer (PS-1), and that prediction is worthless if a grid failure (PS-9) can strand the fleet anyway. GridPulse is deliberately architected as the single control plane that the three problem statements collectively describe.

03

System & Agentic Architecture

Built as a deliberate extension of AWS's own published EV-charging reference architecture — not a reinvention of it.

AWS publishes a canonical reference architecture for EV charging management: device telemetry over MQTT/OCPP into AWS IoT Core, processed by AWS Lambda, with state, the asset registry, balancing configuration and grid-capacity parameters held in Amazon DynamoDB, transaction workflows orchestrated by AWS Step Functions, and an immutable, cryptographically verifiable transaction log in Amazon QLDB[18,19]. GridPulse adopts this spine verbatim, then adds the three layers the reference design does not contain: a chemistry-agnostic abstraction, an autonomous agentic brain, and a cross-operator settlement ledger.

FIG 1 — GridPulse macro-architecture. Heterogeneous edge assets normalize into one DynamoDB state model; the LangGraph/Cencori loop reads state, decides, and writes reroute + settlement intents back through Step Functions; elizaOS clears value and emits MRV-grade attestations.

3.1 — The Telemetry Ingestion Layer

The core engineering problem at the edge is polymorphism: a lithium swap cabinet reports state-of-charge, state-of-health, pack voltage, cell temperature and cycle count; a hydrogen hub reports tank pressure (PSI), mass flow rate, gas purity, leak concentration and compressor duty cycle. These are physically incommensurable signals. GridPulse resolves this with a discriminated-union telemetry envelope: a shared outer schema (identity, geo, power source, signature) wraps a chemistry-specific metrics block selected by an assetClass discriminator. Devices publish over MQTT to AWS IoT Core; an IoT Rule routes the payload to a Lambda normalizer that validates the device certificate, converts both chemistries into a common kWh-equivalent energy unit, and writes to DynamoDB. This is the same IoT Core → Lambda → DynamoDB ingestion path AWS has stress-tested at ~25 million messages/day and 50× current load on a comparable EV platform[19] — meaning GridPulse inherits proven scale characteristics from day one.

3.2 — The LangGraph + Cencori Orchestration Engine

The orchestration engine is a stateful graph, not a stateless rules table. Cencori manages the lifecycle and concurrency of LangGraph runs; LangGraph holds the run state as a typed object that is mutated as it passes between nodes and checkpointed to DynamoDB at every transition, so a run survives a Lambda timeout or cold start. A canonical autonomous cycle on a localized grid failure proceeds as follows:

FIG 2 — The autonomous rebalancing loop. The agent crosses chemistry boundaries when allocating (a stranded lithium fleet can be rerouted to a hydrogen hub measured in shared kWh-equivalent), and only escalates to a human when a reroute volume or settlement value crosses a configured safety threshold.

The decisive design choice is that ALLOCATE is chemistry-blind. Because both asset classes are reduced to a common kWh-equivalent, the agent can route a depleted lithium fleet to a hydrogen hub if that is the nearest viable energy — something impossible in any single-operator, single-chemistry system. Human intervention is not required for the loop to close; a configurable gate intercepts only decisions whose blast radius (reroute volume or settlement value) exceeds policy, satisfying PS-9's "without human intervention" requirement while preserving an auditable safety boundary.

3.3 — The elizaOS Tokenized Settlement Ledger

Cross-operator routing creates an immediate economic problem: if Operator A's fleet draws energy from Operator B's hub, B must be paid — automatically, instantly, and without trusting A. GridPulse's settlement layer uses elizaOS native wallets to give each node and fleet an autonomous machine identity capable of holding value and signing transactions. Settlement is proof-gated: a payment intent is only cleared when the underlying energy delivery is corroborated by signed IoT hardware telemetry from the dispensing node (the verifiable PSI drop or pack-discharge that physically occurred). This is the same trust pattern AWS encodes with Amazon QLDB's cryptographically verifiable transaction log[18] — GridPulse externalizes it onto an inter-operator ledger so that competing businesses can transact. Every cleared settlement also writes an immutable kWh/MJ clean-energy record, which becomes the raw material for carbon and green-bond verification (§6).

Statement of Work & Technical Scope

Exact operational boundaries of the MVP delivered inside the 24-hour sprint.

▲ IN SCOPE — What we built
  • Polymorphic telemetry simulator — a Python generator emitting realistic lithium and hydrogen node payloads (including injected grid-failure events) to AWS IoT Core.
  • Ingestion + normalization Lambda — validates, converts both chemistries to kWh-equivalent, and persists to the DynamoDB polymorphic state model.
  • Cencori-managed LangGraph routing loop — the ASSESS→FORECAST→ALLOCATE→REROUTE→SETTLE cycle with DynamoDB checkpointing and the human-gate threshold.
  • Stateful DynamoDB single-table schema — nodes, operators, rebalancing runs and settlement intents in one queryable model with a cluster-availability GSI.
  • elizaOS settlement ledger prototype — proof-gated M2M settlement intents and an immutable clean-energy ledger export endpoint.
  • GridPulse REST API + minimal ops console — the public surface plus a read-only dashboard to drive the live demo.
▼ OUT OF SCOPE — Future iterations
  • Physical locker / hardware integration — no real cabinet actuation or hydrogen valve control during the sprint; telemetry is simulated.
  • National grid SCADA integration — grid-failure signals are simulated, not pulled from a live DisCo/SCADA feed.
  • Consumer-facing mobile app — GridPulse is strictly a B2B infrastructure layer; rider UX belongs to the operators who consume the API.
  • On-chain mainnet settlement — settlement runs against a testnet/sandbox; production custody, KYC and treasury controls are deferred.
  • ML model training pipeline — the forecasting node uses a heuristic/seeded model in the MVP; a trained demand model is a roadmap item.

4.2 — Data Models & Agent State Boundaries

DynamoDB uses a single-table polymorphic design. Partition/sort keys: NODE#<id> with sort keys META, STATE#latest, TELEMETRY#<ts>; OPERATOR#<id>; REBAL#<runId> with STATE and EVENT#<ts> items; SETTLE#<id>. A single GSI keyed on cluster powers the availability API. The agent's state boundary is explicit: the LangGraph run owns the rebalancing decision and writes only intents (reroutes, source-shifts, settlement intents) — it never directly actuates hardware or moves funds, which keeps the autonomous layer auditable and reversible.

The state object is the contract between nodes. Each node receives it, mutates only its owned fields, and returns it; the checkpointer persists the mutation before the next node runs. This makes the autonomous decision replayable and fully auditable — a property the settlement and carbon-verification layers depend on.

Business Impact, Scalability & Vision

From recovered rider-hours to internationally bankable clean-energy collateral.

6.1 — Operational Cost Reduction (transparent model)

For a commercial e-mobility fleet, downtime is the dominant variable cost: a rider who cannot swap or refuel does not earn, and the operator's asset-financing obligation does not pause. GridPulse attacks this directly by collapsing stock-out probability and eliminating manual outage response. The model below is illustrative and assumption-driven — presented so the method, not a marketing figure, is what persuades:

Assume a 1,000-vehicle fleet · 8 earning-hours/day · ₦1,800 net margin/rider-hour
Baseline stock-out + outage downtime ≈ 6% of earning-hours lost
With GridPulse predictive pre-positioning + autonomous reroute → modelled 4 pts recovered
Recovered ≈ 1,000 × 8 × 0.04 × ₦1,800 ≈ ₦576,000 / day ≈ ₦210M / year / 1,000 vehicles

Against MAX-class ambitions of supporting hundreds of thousands of drivers[13], even conservative per-fleet recovery compounds into network-level economics that change the unit cost of African e-mobility. The point is not the exact naira figure — it is that availability is now an engineered, measurable SLA rather than an act of faith.

6.2 — The Ledger as Bankable Carbon Collateral

The single hardest part of issuing carbon credits or structuring a green bond in this market is verification: proving, to an international standard, that the clean energy was actually delivered. GridPulse produces this as a by-product. Every settled energy transfer writes an immutable, signed kWh/MJ record tied to verifiable IoT hardware proof — exactly the Measurement, Reporting & Verification (MRV) artifact a credit registry or bond auditor needs. This converts a fragmented, paper-based operator network into one that can produce machine-verifiable, audit-grade environmental attestations on demand, unlocking carbon-credit revenue and green-bond financing that are otherwise inaccessible. This mirrors the systems-level, energy-and-economics framing that institutions such as Stanford's new Sustainable Mobility Center[1] and Harvard's urban-mobility climate initiative[2] argue the sector now requires.

6.3 — Horizontal Scalability Across Sub-Saharan Africa

1

Serverless by default

IoT Core fleet provisioning, DynamoDB on-demand capacity, and Lambda concurrency scale per-cluster with zero re-architecture — the same pattern AWS has validated at 50× load on comparable EV telemetry[19].

2

Cluster, then city

The unit of expansion is the cluster (Ikeja, Yaba…), then the city (Ibadan, Kano), then the corridor (Accra, Kumasi, Nairobi). Each new node simply registers and begins emitting the standard envelope.

3

Operator-agnostic onboarding

Because the abstraction is chemistry- and operator-blind, GridPulse can absorb a new franchise — lithium or hydrogen, incumbent or startup — without bespoke integration, growing the shared marketplace network-effect with each join.

07

Novelty — The Defensible Distinction

What makes GridPulse architecturally new, and not a dashboard with extra steps.

Existing EV-energy platforms monitor; GridPulse governs. The defensibility rests on three compounding firsts, each of which is hard to copy because they only work together.

01

Chemistry-agnostic abstraction

The first layer to model a lithium swap cabinet and a hydrogen canister hub as the same kind of thing — interchangeable kWh-equivalent energy nodes. This is the "software-defined energy" primitive everything else builds on.

02

Autonomous agentic rebalancing

A stateful LangGraph/Cencori loop that decides and acts on grid failure — rerouting fleets across operators and across chemistries without a human — rather than surfacing an alert for someone to handle later.

03

Trustless cross-operator settlement

Proof-gated elizaOS M2M settlement lets competitors share infrastructure safely. This dissolves the coordination barrier — distrust between operators — that keeps the market siloed today.

The kicker for these judges: GridPulse is the autonomous, multi-chemistry, multi-operator generalization of the exact EV-charging reference architecture AWS already publishes and endorses[18,19,20]. It speaks AWS's own architectural language — and then extends it into territory the reference design explicitly leaves open. That is the difference between a hackathon demo and a credible piece of infrastructure.

Risk Management

Engineering trade-offs named honestly, with the mitigation already designed in.

RiskSeverityMitigation
Autonomous agent makes a bad reroute / hallucinated decisionHIGHAgent writes intents only — never directly actuates. Configurable human-gate intercepts high-blast-radius decisions; every step is checkpointed and replayable for audit.
Telemetry spoofing / fraudulent energy claimsHIGHDevice-certificate signing at IoT Core; settlement is proof-gated against signed hardware telemetry, so a claim with no corroborating PSI/discharge proof never clears.
Hydrogen handling safety (pressure, leak)MEDLeak-ppm and pressure thresholds are first-class telemetry fields; nodes breaching safety bounds are removed from the candidate set before ALLOCATE ever considers them.
Live grid-failure signal unavailable (no SCADA)MEDMVP uses a simulated, injectable grid-failure event; the trigger interface is abstracted so a real DisCo/SCADA feed drops in without touching the agent logic.
